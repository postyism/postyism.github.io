healing sketchbook (2019)

illustration. graphite on paper, hand stitched sketchbook

↩︎ return_

this has been a very personal project.

_
content warning: illness, mental health
in the spring of 2019, i had gone through a life altering event.
let's just say, having been luckily mostly ablebodied for many years and still working in visual arts, you wouldn't wish to lose an eye.

_
a retinal detachment happened.
very unintended, out of nowhere. and the healing journey took many turns, many months. with the lack of peer support and the distance between me and my hometown family at that time, it once became the most isolating and traumatizing experience. i was led to think about accessibility needs, community support, and healing of the minds. the desire to become trees, holding other trees, roots tangled.
thanks to the amazing doctors and the equally good universal health care, i eventually regained health and vision. not 20/20, but stable enough. finally being able to see and draw again was one of the greatest feelings. later on in the year, i took the time to slowly get used to my brand new warped vision, the light orbs and glitches, and the permantly (hopefully not) enlarged left pupil, while processing the sentiments piled up from the past several bed-bound months.
_
here are these sentiments, written on paper, scanned and presented to you.
